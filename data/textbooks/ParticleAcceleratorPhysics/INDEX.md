## Chapters

### ch01_introduction.md (lines 1--776 of source)

Introduces particle accelerator systems and their main components, including particle sources, injectors, linear and circular accelerators, and storage rings. Covers the definitions and unit systems used throughout the text, provides a primer on special relativity including Lorentz transformations, 4-vectors, relativistic kinematics, and the energy-momentum relation. Lays the foundation for all subsequent beam dynamics discussions.

**Sections index**: [ch01_introduction_SECTIONS.md](ch01_introduction_SECTIONS.md)

---

### ch02_linear_accelerators.md (lines 777--1031 of source)

Covers the principles of linear accelerators including electrostatic acceleration, charged particles in electric fields, and the derivation of transformation matrices for iris electrodes and electrode doublets. Discusses the Alvarez structure, rf linac principles, and the conditions for efficient energy transfer from rf fields to particles.

**Sections index**: [ch02_linear_accelerators_SECTIONS.md](ch02_linear_accelerators_SECTIONS.md)

---

### ch03_circular_accelerators.md (lines 1032--1431 of source)

Discusses circular accelerator types including the betatron, cyclotron, synchrocyclotron, and synchrotron, deriving the fundamental operating principles for each. Introduces adiabatic damping and the concept of phase focusing. Covers the transition from weak to strong focusing and the basic design philosophy of modern synchrotrons and storage rings.

**Sections index**: [ch03_circular_accelerators_SECTIONS.md](ch03_circular_accelerators_SECTIONS.md)

---

### ch04_classical_mechanics.md (lines 1432--1782 of source)

Develops the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formalism of classical mechanics as the theoretical foundation for beam dynamics. Derives the Euler-Lagrange equations, canonical transformations, and the Hamiltonian equations of motion. Formulates the relativistic Hamiltonian for charged particles in electromagnetic fields, establishing the framework used throughout the rest of the text.

**Sections index**: [ch04_classical_mechanics_SECTIONS.md](ch04_classical_mechanics_SECTIONS.md)

---

### ch05_particle_dynamics_em_fields.md (lines 1783--2277 of source)

Derives the equations of motion for charged particles under the Lorentz force, establishing the fundamentals of beam optics. Covers particle beam guidance by dipole fields, beam focusing by quadrupole fields, and the concept of the magnet lattice. Introduces the coordinate system and formalism used for describing particle trajectories in beam transport systems.

**Sections index**: [ch05_particle_dynamics_em_fields_SECTIONS.md](ch05_particle_dynamics_em_fields_SECTIONS.md)

---

### ch06_electromagnetic_fields.md (lines 2278--3118 of source)

Provides a comprehensive treatment of magnetic multipole fields from the Laplace equation. Derives the pure multipole field expansion, including dipole, quadrupole, sextupole, and higher-order fields using complex potential methods. Develops the full equations of motion in curvilinear coordinates including all nonlinear perturbation terms, and discusses general electromagnetic field properties relevant to beam transport.

**Sections index**: [ch06_electromagnetic_fields_SECTIONS.md](ch06_electromagnetic_fields_SECTIONS.md)

---

### ch07_single_particle_dynamics.md (lines 3119--3770 of source)

Opens Part III (Beam Dynamics) with the theory of linear single-particle dynamics. Derives transformation matrices for drift spaces, quadrupoles, and bending magnets, and introduces the matrix formalism for beam transport. Discusses strong focusing, the thin-lens approximation, quadrupole doublets and triplets, and the principles of linear beam optics used in designing beam transport lines and circular accelerators.

**Sections index**: [ch07_single_particle_dynamics_SECTIONS.md](ch07_single_particle_dynamics_SECTIONS.md)

---

### ch08_particle_beams_phase_space.md (lines 3771--4503 of source)

Introduces the phase space description of particle beams, defining beam emittance and proving Liouville's theorem for conservative systems. Derives the betatron functions (Twiss parameters), the Courant-Snyder invariant, and the phase ellipse formalism. Covers beam matrix methods, action-angle variables, and the relationship between single-particle dynamics and collective beam properties.

**Sections index**: [ch08_particle_beams_phase_space_SECTIONS.md](ch08_particle_beams_phase_space_SECTIONS.md)

---

### ch09_longitudinal_beam_dynamics.md (lines 4504--5280 of source)

Treats particle acceleration by rf fields, deriving the equations of longitudinal motion and phase stability. Introduces the concept of the synchronous particle, synchrotron oscillations, and rf buckets (both stationary and moving). Discusses phase space dynamics, momentum compaction, and the transition energy, establishing the conditions for stable particle acceleration in rf cavities.

**Sections index**: [ch09_longitudinal_beam_dynamics_SECTIONS.md](ch09_longitudinal_beam_dynamics_SECTIONS.md)

### ch10_periodic_focusing_systems.md (lines 5281--6195 of source)

Develops the theory of periodic magnet lattices, with the FODO cell as the primary example. Derives stability criteria, betatron functions, dispersion, and matching conditions for periodic structures. Covers the Collins insertion for creating long straight sections, general lattice design principles, and the methods for combining periodic cells into complete accelerator lattices.

**Sections index**: [ch10_periodic_focusing_systems_SECTIONS.md](ch10_periodic_focusing_systems_SECTIONS.md)

### ch11_particle_beam_parameters.md (lines 6196--7072 of source)

Opens Part IV (Beam Parameters) with definitions of key beam parameters including energy, beam current, time structure, and beam sizes. Discusses the characterization of particle beams using beam emittance, betatron functions, and the beam matrix. Covers luminosity, beam-beam interactions, and introduces synchrotron radiation effects including energy loss and damping for electron beams.

**Sections index**: [ch11_particle_beam_parameters_SECTIONS.md](ch11_particle_beam_parameters_SECTIONS.md)

### ch12_vlasov_fokker_planck.md (lines 7073--7730 of source)

Derives the Vlasov equation for collisionless particle distributions in phase space, proving the conservation of phase space density. Applies the Vlasov formalism to obtain damping decrements for synchrotron and betatron oscillations in electron accelerators. Derives the Fokker-Planck equation for systems with damping and diffusion, obtaining stationary solutions that describe equilibrium distributions.

**Sections index**: [ch12_vlasov_fokker_planck_SECTIONS.md](ch12_vlasov_fokker_planck_SECTIONS.md)

### ch13_equilibrium_particle_dist.md (lines 7731--8095 of source)

Discusses equilibrium particle distributions in phase space, focusing on the interplay between quantum excitation from synchrotron radiation and radiation damping. Derives the diffusion coefficient from photon emission, the equilibrium energy spread, equilibrium bunch length, and the transverse equilibrium beam emittance determined by dispersion and radiation effects.

**Sections index**: [ch13_equilibrium_particle_dist_SECTIONS.md](ch13_equilibrium_particle_dist_SECTIONS.md)

### ch14_beam_emittance_lattice.md (lines 8096--8311 of source)

Addresses lattice design optimization for minimum beam emittance in electron storage rings. Derives scaling laws for the equilibrium emittance as a function of lattice parameters and bending magnet properties. Discusses the evolution of synchrotron radiation sources from first through fourth generation, including the role of insertion devices and the transition to free electron laser facilities.

**Sections index**: [ch14_beam_emittance_lattice_SECTIONS.md](ch14_beam_emittance_lattice_SECTIONS.md)

### ch15_perturbations_beam_dynamics.md (lines 8312--9452 of source)

Treats the effects of magnet misalignments, field errors, and higher-order multipole perturbations on beam dynamics. Derives closed orbit distortions, the dispersion function from dipole errors, quadrupole gradient errors and tune shifts, and chromaticity with its correction by sextupole magnets. Establishes statistical tolerance criteria for magnet alignment and field quality in practical accelerator systems.

**Sections index**: [ch15_perturbations_beam_dynamics_SECTIONS.md](ch15_perturbations_beam_dynamics_SECTIONS.md)

### ch16_resonances.md (lines 9453--9936 of source)

Derives the theory of lattice resonances in circular accelerators, showing how periodic perturbations from multipole field errors can drive particle oscillations unstable. Introduces the resonance diagram (tune diagram), resonance conditions, stop-band widths, and third-order resonances driven by sextupole fields. Discusses fixed points, separatrices, and the practical choice of operating tunes.

**Sections index**: [ch16_resonances_SECTIONS.md](ch16_resonances_SECTIONS.md)

### ch17_hamiltonian_nonlinear.md (lines 9937--10520 of source)

Extends the treatment of beam dynamics to nonlinear effects using Hamiltonian perturbation theory. Discusses chromatic and geometric aberrations from higher-order multipole fields, derives general solutions for perturbation-driven orbit and tune shifts, and formulates the Hamiltonian perturbation theory applicable to the analysis of nonlinear beam dynamics in storage rings and transport lines.

**Sections index**: [ch17_hamiltonian_nonlinear_SECTIONS.md](ch17_hamiltonian_nonlinear_SECTIONS.md)

### ch18_charged_particle_accel.md (lines 10521--11222 of source)

Discusses rf waveguides and cavity design for particle acceleration, deriving the wave equation and boundary conditions for accelerating field modes. Covers the properties of cylindrical rf cavities, the concept of shunt impedance and quality factor, energy gain and transit time factor, and the coupling of rf power to accelerating structures. Provides the technical basis for understanding practical rf systems.

**Sections index**: [ch18_charged_particle_accel_SECTIONS.md](ch18_charged_particle_accel_SECTIONS.md)

### ch19_beam_cavity_interaction.md (lines 11223--11758 of source)

Analyzes the interaction between a particle beam and rf cavities, modeling the cavity as a parallel resonant circuit driven by both generator and beam currents. Derives beam loading effects, the coupling coefficient, and Robinson instability conditions. Discusses the optimization of rf power transfer and the stability criteria for combined generator-cavity-beam systems.

**Sections index**: [ch19_beam_cavity_interaction_SECTIONS.md](ch19_beam_cavity_interaction_SECTIONS.md)

### ch20_dynamics_coupled_motion.md (lines 11759--12367 of source)

Opens Part VI with the treatment of linear coupling between horizontal and vertical motion caused by skew quadrupole fields and solenoid magnets. Derives the 4x4 transformation matrices for coupled systems, analyzes particle motion in solenoidal fields, and develops the formalism for understanding and compensating coupling effects in beam transport systems and circular accelerators.

**Sections index**: [ch20_dynamics_coupled_motion_SECTIONS.md](ch20_dynamics_coupled_motion_SECTIONS.md)

### ch21_statistical_collective.md (lines 12368--13018 of source)

Opens Part VIII (Intense Beams) with a discussion of statistical and collective effects in particle beams. Covers Schottky noise, the Touschek effect (intrabeam scattering leading to beam lifetime limitations), and space charge forces including both direct and image charge effects. Derives the tune shift from space charge and its implications for beam stability in high-intensity machines.

**Sections index**: [ch21_statistical_collective_SECTIONS.md](ch21_statistical_collective_SECTIONS.md)

### ch22_wake_fields_instabilities.md (lines 13019--14214 of source)

Provides a comprehensive treatment of wake fields, impedances, and beam instabilities in accelerators. Defines longitudinal and transverse wake fields and impedances, derives parasitic mode losses, and analyzes coasting-beam instabilities (negative mass instability) and bunched-beam instabilities. Applies the Vlasov equation to derive stability criteria including Landau damping, the Keil-Schnell criterion, and microwave instabilities.

**Sections index**: [ch22_wake_fields_instabilities_SECTIONS.md](ch22_wake_fields_instabilities_SECTIONS.md)

### ch23_fundamental_processes.md (lines 14215--14389 of source)

Opens Part IX (Synchrotron Radiation) with the fundamental physics of electromagnetic radiation from moving charges. Discusses retarded potentials, the physical origin of radiation from accelerated charges, and the distinction between spontaneous synchrotron radiation from bending magnets, undulators, and wigglers. Provides conceptual foundations for the detailed theory in subsequent chapters.

**Sections index**: [ch23_fundamental_processes_SECTIONS.md](ch23_fundamental_processes_SECTIONS.md)

### ch24_synchrotron_radiation.md (lines 14390--15061 of source)

Gives a quantitative overview of synchrotron radiation properties including radiation sources (bending magnets, superbends, wavelength shifters, wigglers, undulators), total radiation power, angular distribution, critical photon energy, and the universal spectral distribution function. Derives the energy loss per turn, discusses radiation from insertion devices, and introduces the spectral brightness as a figure of merit.

**Sections index**: [ch24_synchrotron_radiation_SECTIONS.md](ch24_synchrotron_radiation_SECTIONS.md)

### ch25_theory_synchrotron_rad.md (lines 15062--15808 of source)

Develops the formal electromagnetic theory of synchrotron radiation starting from the Lienard-Wiechert potentials. Derives the radiation field of a relativistic point charge, the angular and spectral distribution of radiation using Airy functions and modified Bessel functions, and the polarization properties. Covers both the radiation field in the time domain and the frequency domain spectral analysis.

**Sections index**: [ch25_theory_synchrotron_rad_SECTIONS.md](ch25_theory_synchrotron_rad_SECTIONS.md)

### ch26_insertion_device_radiation.md (lines 15809--16324 of source)

Derives in detail the radiation characteristics of periodic magnetic insertion devices (undulators and wigglers). Covers particle dynamics in sinusoidal fields, the undulator equation relating radiation wavelength to period and field strength, the distinction between undulator and wiggler regimes, harmonic content, angular distribution, and the spectral brightness of insertion device radiation.

**Sections index**: [ch26_insertion_device_radiation_SECTIONS.md](ch26_insertion_device_radiation_SECTIONS.md)

### ch27_free_electron_lasers.md (lines 16325--16682 of source)

Introduces the principles of the free electron laser (FEL), explaining the stimulated emission process in undulators using the optical cavity recycling concept. Covers the small gain (Madey theorem) and high gain regimes, derives the FEL gain formula, and discusses Self-Amplified Spontaneous Emission (SASE) for single-pass X-ray FELs. Describes the electron beam requirements and facility design considerations.

**Sections index**: [ch27_free_electron_lasers_SECTIONS.md](ch27_free_electron_lasers_SECTIONS.md)
